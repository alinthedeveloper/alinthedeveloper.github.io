Tx2Day

Summer 2023 - Present

Occupational therapy paperwork automation webapp

×

Tx2Day.com

Overview:

Tx2Day is a web application I've been working on aimed at simplifying therapy documentation processes for practitioners. In particular, the application is designed to help Occupational Therapists manage their daily reports, client information, and scheduling needs.

Goal:

The primary goal of the Tx2Day project is to create a user-friendly platform that addresses the complexities of therapy documentation by building a modular daily schedule and report interface.

Insights & Skills:

UI/UX:

Throughout the project, I had the opportunity to leverage and showcase the user experience design skills I acquired during my studies in the Boise State University GIMM Degree. By utilizing paper prototypes in conjunction with regular client meetings, I gained real world experience designing and iterating on a series of prototypes to determine what experience would best fit the interface.

Technical:

Outside of the sphere of designed interactions, this project has also introduced me to a series of common technical issues and solutions in the web development world. In order to ensure the confidentiality of client data, I have learned how to implement data encryption within SQL queries, build a secure login system, and implement a series of data persistence solutions to ensure that client data is not lost between sessions.

What I enjoyed about the project:

What makes working on the Tx2Day project particularly enjoyable for me thus far is that I have been given an opportunity to learn every step it takes to make a solid, structured web application. This project has essentially been my first forray into Web Development, and from setting up a hosting service to building a comprehensive registration system, I've become a much better programmer while working on this project.

Sappy

September 6, 2024 - November 7, 2024

3D Platformer

×

Sappy, a sapling story

Overview:

Sappy is a 3D platformer that a small team and I made for one of my club's (Game Development and Design Club at Boise State) game jam's. The game is about a seedling that has to grow through different "sunspots" in order to get back to his family after being blown away.

Goal:

For this project, the game jam's theme was Growth, so our goal for the project was to find an interesting way to build that into the theme of the game.

Contributions

Leadership

For this project, I was the project manager, facilitated meetings, structured check-ins, and ensured that progress continued throughout the timeframe.

Programming

I built out the animation controller for sappy, figured out how the insect movement would be implemented, set up dynamic audio rules, created the pause menu mechanic, and worked on other general debugging.

Audio

I created all of the music for the game in Ableton Live 11.

Level Design

In the early stages of development, Jordan Alba Reed and I generated terrain for the open world using heightmap data from around the Boise area and Terrain Export tools. This grayboxing work was then sent to the 3D artist on our team, Jaden Rogers, who took what we made and stylized it into the final terrain model that we used in the game.

What I enjoyed about the project:

I think the most enjoyable aspect of working on the Sappy project for me was that it wasn't bound to the same rules as my school assignments. During the time where I was working on this project, I was also a 3D artist for 4 other projects, and having the opportunity to work on something outside of Blender was a nice change of pace. I also had a lot of fun working with the team I was on, and I think that the final product is a great representation of what we were able to accomplish in such a short time frame.

Entangled

Spring 2023

Interactive comic about quantum entanglement

×

Entangled Interactive Comic

Overview:

Entangled is an interactive comic four classmates and I created in our first year at GIMM. The comic is an allegory for the concept of quantum entanglement, and is designed to be a fun and engaging way to learn about the topic.

Goal:

The primary goal we were given for the Entangled project was to find a way to introduce and explain the idea of quantum entanglement through the medium of a web comic. The comic is designed to be accessible to a wide audience, and our primary method of achieving this was through showing, rather than telling the basic fundamental rules of the concept.

Contributions:

Programming

Another student and I took up the brunt of the coding challenges in this project. I personally tackled building out a "Dating Game" that randomly instantiates an array of Unity Prefabs using a set series of locations and deletes them when entangled "partners" are clicked in sequence. I also helped build out a panel changing system that moved the camera between comic panels, added tap interactions, built out the credit scene, and helped with debugging.

Art and Animations

I used Aseprite to animate different dancing animations for each of the entangled pairs, created sprites for the main character, and used photoshop for rough drafting comic panels. I also used Adobe AfterEffects to edit the final video that plays at the end of the comic.

Storytelling

Near the start of this project's development period once we had decided on using soulmates as a metaphor for entangled particles, I wrote out a script of the story that we used in order to plan out the panels.

Audio

I created a song for the dating game using Ableton Live 11 and Serum.

What I enjoyed about the project:

Outside of it being classwork, I had a great time working on this project and have presented the final product at Hackfort (within the Boise Treefort festival) for the last couple of years now. I wrote the story that our final product is based on, and the team I worked with have become some of my closest friends in my degree, so this project is very close to my heart.

Card Wizard

Fall 2022

Mixed reality top-down shooter

×

AR Top Down Shooter

Overview:

Card Wizard is a mixed reality game that I and two of my classmates developed in Unity during our first semester within the GIMM degree.

Goal:

The goal of Card Wizard was to create a protoype for a competitive multiplayer game where one individual controls the enemies, and the other individual controls the player and tries to defeat them.

Contributions:

Programming

I was responsible for building out the projectile mechanics, an audio manager, and made a multitude of bug fixes for this project. The most noteworthy of this list for me was how I build the audio manager, which was a modular system that allowed for simple implementation of sound effects though an interface I built. I also used some trigonometry to show the player where they were shooting by having a target that would circle around the player based on the position of the mouse on the screen in comparison to the player character.

Art and Animations

I created multiple characters and animations within Aseprite that can be seen below.

Audio

I created all the sound effects and music that was used in this project within Ableton Live 11

UI

I also developed a series of UI sprites that we didn't end up implementing in the final project, but would have brought more personality to the game had we had more time.

What I enjoyed about the project:

The thing I enjoyed most within my time working on this project was probably working on the boss animations. Though we had to scrap them for our final timeline, I had a lot of fun and learned a lot about building out bigger animations for a boss character during that process.
