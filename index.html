The artifact is about my work on the website / webapp Tx2Day, which is a new way to approach therapy documentation. The webapp is focused on simplifying the documentation of visits and the hassle of scheduling for government-backed therapy businesses. While working on this project, I had the opportunity to showcase the user experience design skills I learned in my first year of GIMM in meetings with clients. This project was fun to build because I had multiple chances for client feedback while designing the face of Tx2Day and was able to incorporate what they wanted into the design while staying away from bland theming.

The Cave

(2D Platformer)

The artifact is about showcasing my first experience delving into game design and showcasing my art and storytelling style. The game is a spin off of the first half of Plato’s Allegory of the Cave and figuring out how to translate the ideas present in that text was the highlight of my experience. I learned so much about how game engines work and how to solve problems I was having in order to integrate new mechanics into my game from this project. The project was fun to build because it was my brainchild. I made every aspect of it and ideating about all the ways the game could go was a great exercise in having a good idea and seeing how far it can take you.

AR Top Down Shooter

In this project, my team and I were challenged to create an experience involving AR. We decided to create a Top Down Shooter game with one player controlling the character through the computer’s Keyboard and mouse, and another player controlling the enemies and boss using Cards that we created. I learned a lot about the workflow of building a larger scale project on a team in a short amount of time during this project. The project was fun to build because I had the opportunity to create some cool sounds, sprites, and an Audio Statemachine that could be used to play sound effects when bullets were fired or the dash button was pressed.
